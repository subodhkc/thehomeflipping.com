## Understanding Flip Financing

### Why Traditional Banks Don't Work for Flipping

Traditional mortgages are designed for owner-occupants, not investors. Here's why they fail for flipping:

| Factor | Traditional Mortgage | Flip Financing Need |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Approval Time | 30-45 days | 7-14 days |
| Property Condition | Must be habitable | Often distressed |
| Income Verification | Extensive | Asset-based |
| Number of Loans | Limited (usually 4-10) | Unlimited |
| Renovation Funds | Not included | Built into loan |

### Types of Flip Financing

1. **Hard Money Loans** - Asset-based, fast, higher rates
2. **Private Money** - Individual investors, negotiable terms
3. **DSCR Loans** - Debt Service Coverage Ratio loans
4. **Home Equity** - Use equity in existing property
5. **Business Lines of Credit** - Revolving credit
6. **Partnerships** - Split profits for capital
7. **Seller Financing** - Owner carries the note
8. **Self-Directed IRA** - Use retirement funds

## Hard Money Lenders Explained

### What is Hard Money?

Hard money loans are short-term, asset-based loans secured by real estate. The "hard" refers to the hard asset (the property) rather than your credit score or income.

### Key Characteristics

| Feature | Typical Terms |
|---------|---------------|
| Loan Term | 6-18 months |
| Interest Rate | 10-15% annually |
| Points (Origination Fee) | 2-4 points (2-4% of loan) |
| LTV (Loan-to-Value) | 65-75% of ARV |
| LTC (Loan-to-Cost) | 80-90% of purchase + rehab |
| Closing Time | 7-14 days |
| Prepayment Penalty | Usually none |

### How Hard Money Differs from Traditional Lending

**Traditional Bank Loan:**
- Based on YOUR creditworthiness
- Requires income verification, tax returns
- 30-45 day closing
- Low rates (6-8%)
- Long terms (15-30 years)
- Property must be in good condition

**Hard Money Loan:**
- Based on the PROPERTY'S value
- Minimal income verification
- 7-14 day closing
- Higher rates (10-15%)
- Short terms (6-18 months)
- Distressed properties OK

### When to Use Hard Money

**Ideal Situations:**
- Auction purchases requiring fast cash
- Distressed properties banks won't finance
- When you need to close quickly (competitive market)
- Fix and flip projects
- Bridge financing between properties
- When traditional financing isn't available

**Not Ideal For:**
- Long-term holds (rates too high)
- Properties you plan to live in
- Deals with thin margins (costs eat profit)

## How to Get Hard Money Loans

### Step 1: Find Hard Money Lenders

**National Hard Money Lenders:**
- Lima One Capital
- Kiavi (formerly LendingHome)
- RCN Capital
- Visio Lending
- CoreVest
- Groundfloor
- Fund That Flip

**How to Find Local Lenders:**
1. Google "[Your City] hard money lender"
2. Ask at local REIA meetings
3. Network with other investors
4. Ask real estate agents who work with investors
5. Check BiggerPockets forums

### Step 2: Prepare Your Application

**What Hard Money Lenders Want:**

1. **The Deal (Most Important)**
   - Property address
   - Purchase price
   - ARV with comparable sales
   - Detailed rehab budget
   - Timeline
   - Exit strategy

2. **Your Experience**
   - Previous flips completed
   - Real estate background
   - Contractor relationships

3. **Financial Position**
   - Proof of funds for down payment
   - Reserves (usually 3-6 months of payments)
   - Credit score (some require 620+, others don't check)

4. **Entity Information**
   - LLC operating agreement
   - EIN number
   - Business bank account

### Step 3: Submit a Strong Package

**Deal Package Checklist:**

- [ ] Property photos (exterior, interior, problem areas)
- [ ] Purchase contract
- [ ] 3-5 comparable sales within 1 mile, 6 months
- [ ] Detailed scope of work with line-item costs
- [ ] Contractor bids (if available)
- [ ] ARV justification
- [ ] Timeline for renovation
- [ ] Exit strategy (sell or refinance)
- [ ] Proof of funds for down payment
- [ ] Personal financial statement
- [ ] Previous project portfolio (if applicable)

### Step 4: Understand the Terms

**Questions to Ask Every Lender:**

1. What is your interest rate?
2. How many points do you charge?
3. What is the maximum LTV/LTC?
4. Do you fund rehab costs? How are draws handled?
5. What is your minimum credit score requirement?
6. How long does approval take?
7. Are there prepayment penalties?
8. What are the extension fees if I need more time?
9. Do you require personal guarantee?
10. What happens if the project goes over budget?

### Step 5: Close and Execute

**Typical Closing Process:**

1. Submit application and deal package
2. Lender orders appraisal or BPO
3. Receive term sheet (1-3 days)
4. Sign loan documents
5. Title company handles closing
6. Funds disbursed (purchase price)
7. Rehab funds held in escrow
8. Request draws as work completes
9. Lender inspects before releasing draws
10. Complete project, sell, pay off loan

## Private Money Lenders

### What is Private Money?

Private money comes from individuals (not institutions) who lend their personal funds for real estate investments. These are often:

- Friends and family
- Wealthy individuals seeking returns
- Self-directed IRA holders
- Other real estate investors
- Business professionals (doctors, lawyers, etc.)

### Private Money vs Hard Money

| Factor | Private Money | Hard Money |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| Source | Individuals | Companies |
| Terms | Negotiable | Standardized |
| Rates | 6-12% | 10-15% |
| Points | 0-2 | 2-4 |
| Relationship | Personal | Transactional |
| Flexibility | High | Medium |
| Speed | Varies | Fast |

### Finding Private Money Lenders

**Where to Find Them:**

1. **Your Network**
   - Family members with savings
   - Friends with investment capital
   - Colleagues and coworkers
   - Professional contacts

2. **REIA Meetings**
   - Attend local investor meetings
   - Network with passive investors
   - Look for "money partners"

3. **Professional Circles**
   - Doctors, dentists, lawyers
   - Business owners
   - Retirees with savings
   - People frustrated with stock market returns

4. **Online Platforms**
   - BiggerPockets forums
   - LinkedIn real estate groups
   - Local Facebook investor groups

### The Private Money Pitch

**What Private Lenders Want to Hear:**

1. **Security** - Their investment is secured by real estate
2. **Returns** - Better than CDs, bonds, or savings accounts
3. **Passive** - They do nothing, you do the work
4. **Track Record** - You know what you're doing
5. **Exit Strategy** - How and when they get paid back

**Sample Pitch Script:**

> "I'm looking for investment partners who want to earn 8-10% returns secured by real estate. Here's how it works: You lend me $100,000 for a property I'm renovating. Your investment is secured by a first-position lien on the property, just like a bank. The property is worth $180,000 after repairs, so you have plenty of equity protection. In 6 months when I sell, you get your $100,000 back plus $5,000 in interest. You do nothing except collect a check. Would you like to see my current project?"

### Structuring Private Money Deals

**Common Structures:**

1. **Straight Loan**
   - Fixed interest rate (8-12%)
   - Monthly or balloon payment
   - Secured by deed of trust/mortgage

2. **Equity Partnership**
   - Lender provides capital
   - You provide expertise and labor
   - Split profits 50/50 or negotiated

3. **Hybrid**
   - Preferred return (8%) plus profit share
   - Lender gets paid first, then split remaining profit

**Legal Protection for Both Parties:**

- Promissory note (loan terms)
- Deed of trust or mortgage (security)
- Title insurance
- Hazard insurance (lender as loss payee)
- Personal guarantee (optional)

## Traditional Financing Options

### DSCR Loans (Debt Service Coverage Ratio)

**What They Are:**
Loans based on the property's rental income potential, not your personal income.

**Best For:**
- BRRRR strategy (Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat)
- Refinancing after flip if you decide to hold
- Building a rental portfolio

**Typical Terms:**
- 30-year fixed or ARM
- 7-8% interest rates
- 75-80% LTV
- No income verification
- Credit score 660+

### Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)

**What It Is:**
A revolving line of credit secured by equity in your primary residence or other property.

**Advantages:**
- Low interest rates (prime + 1-2%)
- Revolving (reuse as you pay down)
- Interest-only payments available
- No points or origination fees

**Disadvantages:**
- Puts your home at risk
- Limited by available equity
- Can be frozen by bank
- Variable rates

**How to Use for Flipping:**
1. Get HELOC on primary residence
2. Use HELOC for down payment on flip
3. Use hard money for remaining purchase + rehab
4. Sell flip, pay off hard money
5. Pay down HELOC, repeat

### Business Line of Credit

**What It Is:**
Unsecured or secured credit line for your flipping business.

**How to Get One:**
1. Establish LLC with business bank account
2. Build business credit (start with secured cards)
3. Show revenue from flips
4. Apply after 1-2 years in business

**Typical Terms:**
- $50,000 - $250,000 limits
- Prime + 2-5% interest
- Revolving credit
- Annual review

## Creative Financing Strategies

### Subject-To Financing

**What It Is:**
Buying a property "subject to" the existing mortgage. The loan stays in the seller's name, but you take over payments and ownership.

**How It Works:**
1. Find motivated seller with existing mortgage
2. Negotiate to take over their payments
3. Deed transfers to you
4. Original loan stays in seller's name
5. You make payments directly to lender
6. Flip property and pay off loan

**Best For:**
- Sellers behind on payments
- Sellers who need to move quickly
- Properties with low-interest existing loans

**Risks:**
- Due-on-sale clause (lender can call loan due)
- Seller's credit still tied to property
- Requires trust between parties

### Seller Financing

**What It Is:**
The seller acts as the bank and finances your purchase.

**How It Works:**
1. Negotiate purchase price and terms with seller
2. Seller carries a note (you make payments to them)
3. Deed transfers to you
4. You renovate and sell
5. Pay off seller's note at sale

**Typical Terms:**
- 5-10% down payment
- 6-10% interest rate
- 1-5 year balloon
- Monthly payments

**Best For:**
- Sellers who own property free and clear
- Sellers wanting passive income
- Properties that don't qualify for traditional financing

### Partnerships

**Types of Partnerships:**

1. **Money Partner + Work Partner**
   - One provides capital
   - One finds deals and manages project
   - Split profits 50/50

2. **Joint Venture**
   - Both contribute capital
   - Both contribute expertise
   - Split based on contribution

3. **Silent Partner**
   - Investor provides all capital
   - You do all work
   - Investor gets preferred return + profit share

**Partnership Agreement Essentials:**

- Capital contributions
- Roles and responsibilities
- Decision-making authority
- Profit/loss distribution
- Exit strategy
- Dispute resolution
- What happens if project fails

## Funding for Cash Auctions

### The Cash Auction Challenge

Agencies like **New Western**, **Auction.com**, and courthouse auctions require:
- Proof of funds before bidding
- Cash or cashier's check at auction
- Closing in 7-30 days
- No financing contingencies

### Solution 1: Hard Money Pre-Approval

**How It Works:**
1. Get pre-approved with hard money lender
2. Lender provides proof of funds letter
3. Use letter to bid at auctions
4. When you win, lender funds the deal
5. Close in 7-14 days

**What You Need:**
- Relationship with hard money lender
- Down payment ready (10-25%)
- Deal analysis for each property you bid on

### Solution 2: Transactional Funding

**What It Is:**
Very short-term funding (1-3 days) specifically for double closings and assignments.

**How It Works:**
1. You get property under contract at $100,000
2. You find buyer at $120,000
3. Transactional lender funds your purchase
4. Same day: You close with seller, then with buyer
5. Lender gets paid back same day
6. You keep $20,000 profit

**Typical Terms:**
- 1-3 day funding
- 1-2% of loan amount
- No credit check
- Proof of end buyer required

**Transactional Funding Sources:**
- Best Transaction Funding
- Coastal Funding Group
- Fund That Flip
- Local private lenders

### Solution 3: Cash Buyer Partnership

**How It Works:**
1. Find deal at auction
2. Partner with cash buyer to purchase
3. You manage the flip
4. Split profits

**Finding Cash Buyer Partners:**
- REIA meetings
- BiggerPockets
- New Western buyer network
- Local investor Facebook groups

### Solution 4: Proof of Funds Services

**What They Are:**
Services that provide proof of funds letters for a fee, without actually lending you money.

**Important:** These only work if you have actual funding lined up. They're for showing proof to get access to deals, not for actually closing.

**When to Use:**
- Getting on New Western's buyer list
- Accessing off-market deal sources
- Showing sellers you're serious
